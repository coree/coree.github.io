Joseph Cornelius

I am a PhD candidate in the group of Michael Krauthammer at the University of Zurich and University Hospital Zurich, co-supervised by Fabio Rinaldi. I am also a researcher in the NLP group at IDSIA (USI–SUPSI) in Lugano. My research is in natural language processing, with a focus on the scientific, biomedical, biodiversity, and clinical domains, working on knowledge extraction and understanding the differences between human-written and machine-generated text.

I hold an MSc in Neural Systems and Computation from the University of Zurich and ETH Zurich's Institute of Neuroinformatics; I carried out my master's thesis at Stanford as a visiting student researcher, supervised by Noah Goodman and co-supervised by Richard Hahnloser. Before joining IDSIA, I worked as a research assistant at the Department of Computational Linguistics in Zurich. I received a BSc in Computer Science from Heinrich-Heine-University Düsseldorf, where I worked with Stefan Conrad.
